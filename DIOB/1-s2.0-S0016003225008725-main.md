To address the challenge of quadrotor UAVs failing to complete their tasks due to abrupt exter-
nal disturbances and actuator failures, this paper proposes a nonlinear recursive sliding mode 
control (NRSMC) strategy integrated with an adaptive disturbance estimator (ADE) within the 
delta operator framework. First, an adaptive disturbance estimator is designed for the quadrotor 
UAV system in the delta operator framework. This estimator is capable of estimating both the to-
tal disturbance and its derivatives, which enhances the accuracy and adaptability of disturbance 
estimation, particularly in the presence of abrupt disturbances. Second, a nonlinear recursive 
sliding mode control strategy is designed. By combining the disturbance estimation results, the 
control law ensures the system converges quickly and smoothly. Finally, simulation experiments 
are conducted to validate the proposed method. The results show that the approach can eпђАec-
tively compensate for abrupt disturbances and actuator failures. This allows the quadrotor UAV 
to maintain stable trajectory tracking and successfully complete its tasks when facing abrupt dis-
turbances and actuator failures.
1.  Introduction
The quadrotor UAV(Unmanned Aerial Vehicle) has a simple structure, consisting of four symmetrically distributed motors and 
rotors. It can achieve precise three-dimensional motion by adjusting the rotor speeds. Due to its excellent пђВexibility and stability, 
quadcopter drones have gained an important status and are widely used in пђБelds such as the military, agriculture, and industry. Recent 
studies have aпђГrmed its advantages and emphasized their continuously expanding applications. Arabi Aliabad et al. [1] described a 
study focused on identifying and monitoring gardens in urban areas of arid regions using aerial and satellite imagery. Mendu et al. [2] 
described a systematic review of the application of unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) in power line inspections, highlighting current 
innovations, trends, and future prospects. However, when performing complex tasks, unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) are often 
susceptible to abrupt external disturbances (e.g. caused by strong winds or turbulent airпђВow) as well as system faults resulting from 
prolonged operation, such as actuator performance degradation or complete failure. These factors can severely compromise пђВight 
stability, leading to loss of control or even catastrophic crashes. Although there have been many studies on fault-tolerant control of 
UAVs based on sliding mode control, there are still two problems that need to be solved.
вАҐ Existing control methods remain inadequate in addressing abrupt disturbances. For example, some robust control methods may 
generate large instantaneous control actions and overshoot when encountering abrupt disturbances, which is caused by the in-
stantaneous changes of the disturbances.

So far, researchers have invested a signiпђБcant amount of eпђАort in fault-tolerant sliding mode control for quadrotor UAVs. Mai et 
al. [3] presented a UAV model including actuator faults,and presented a robust fault-tolerant approach. Zhou et al. [4] presents a

Several sliding mode control strategies based on disturbance estimators have been proposed, which enable fault-tolerant control for 
quadrotor UAVs, improve control accuracy, and reduce convergence time, thereby providing an eпђАective solution for sliding mode 
fault-tolerant control of UAVs [4вАУ6][16вАУ19].
In order to estimate disturbances more accurately, various adaptive estimators have been proposed. Adaptive estimation of un-
certainty bounds is used for compensation and fault separation [7,8]. Three types of adaptive disturbance estimators have been 
presented, providing a reference value for adaptive estimation [9вАУ11]. Ranjan et al. [12] proposed a пђБxed-time diпђАerentiator to 
estimate outputsвАЩ derivatives, largely compensating for initial condition problems and solving the delay problem. These adaptive 
methods can accurately separate faults or estimate the impact of disturbances and faults on the system.
Delta operators are commonly used in control to convert between continuous and discrete systems. In practical applications, 
the state update of the quadrotor UAV is usually implemented in a discrete system. Most studies still use idealized continuous 
systems, taking discrete data points from continuous signals, but this approach cannot fully capture the systemвАЩs dynamic behavior. 
To achieve more precise state updates and control in digital controllers, an appropriate discretization method is needed to convert 
between continuous and discrete systems. Traditional discrete methods, such as the z-transform and q-operator transformation, can 
suпђАer from accuracy degradation or even system instability at high sampling frequencies. In contrast, the Delta operator has stronger 
robustness and oпђАers signiпђБcant advantages in the consistency and numerical stability of continuous-discrete systems at high sampling 
frequencies. Yang et al. [13] proposes a disturbance estimator based on the Delta operator to address disturbance estimation and 
compensation problems in uncertain systems, extending to Multi-Input Multi-Output (MIMO) nonlinear systems. Ginoya et al. [14] 
utilizes the Delta operator for systems with parameter uncertainties and external nonlinear disturbances, proposing a sliding mode 
estimator and controller that improves the systemвАЩs disturbance rejection capability.

present simulation results and discussions. Finally, Section 8 concludes the paper and suggests directions for future research.

disturbance rejection. Traditional estimation methods, such as conventional disturbance observers, can handle slow-varying distur-

order estimates progressively reпђБne the updates of lower-order terms. Additionally, we introduce an adaptive estimator gain that

8.  Conclusion
This paper addresses the fault-tolerant control problem for quadrotor UAVs and proposes a control strategy that integrates an 
Adaptive Disturbance Estimator (ADE) with Nonlinear Recursive Sliding Mode Control (NRSMC) within the delta operator framework. 
Simulation results demonstrate that, compared with existing studies, the proposed method ensures stable and high-precision trajectory 
tracking for the UAV in the presence of abrupt disturbances and actuator faults. Moreover, the control input remains moderate during 
steady-state operation, eпђАectively handling abrupt disturbances and compound actuator faults while guaranteeing actuator longevity.
However, the controller proposed in this paper is only applicable to the control of a single UAV, and future work could attempt 
to extend it to formation control.
