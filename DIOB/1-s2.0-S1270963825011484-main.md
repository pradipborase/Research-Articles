a b s t r a c t
Quadcopter Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (QUAV) trajectory tracking has a big challenge due to time-varying dynam-
ics, and external disturbances such as wind. In this paper, an Adaptive Model Predictive Control with Extended 
State Observer (AMPCESO) framework is established. Due to the diﬀerences in the bandwidth of velocity and 
attitude loops, it is diﬃcult to use a single one hidden layer network to identify two diﬀerent time scale dy-
namics, therefore, this paper proposes dual single hidden layer neural networks in the predictive model in the 
model predictive control framework. To further strengthen robustness to the external disturbance and the inter-
nal uncertainty caused by the residual identiﬁcation error of dual neural networks, an ESO is introduced and 
its outputs are incorporated into the dual neural network predictive models. The stability of the proposed AM-
PCESO is rigorously proved using the Lyapunov theorem. Numerical simulation and Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL) 
experiment are conducted to validate the correctness of the theoretical analysis and the performance superiority 
of the proposed method.
1.  Introduction
In recent years, the Quadcopter Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (QUAV) 
have attracted widespread attention, because of the wide applications, 
including cargo transportation [1], remote surveillance [2], emergency 
rescue [3], civil convenience [4], as well as in the battle ﬁeld applica-
tion. In the search, reconnaissance and surveillance applications, it is 
expected that the QUAV maintain a constant relative distance with the 
target, namely the standoﬀ distance [5,6]. In this case, the QUAV refer-
ence trajectory is time variant, the environment is continuously chang-
ing, at the same time, the QUAV model inaccuracy makes the situation 
even worse. Therefore, QUAV trajectory tracking is a challenging task 
for both control theory and engineering control technique.
To address the challenges caused by external disturbance and inac-
curate time-varying model, numerous researches have been carried out, 
such as robust Linear Quadratic Regulator control method [7], Particle 
Swarm Optimization - Proportion Integration Diﬀerentiation(PID)[8], 
Sliding Mode Control(SMC)[9], backstepping sliding mode control [10], 
nonlinear hierarchical control [11]. Fixed-time SMC has been proposed 
for ﬂexible spacecraft [12] and rigid spacecraft [13] with uncertain in-
ertia and external disturbances, however, their control gains are dif-
ﬁcult to adjust to avoid controller singularity. Although these studies 
achieve signiﬁcant progress in handling speciﬁc uncertainties, they re-

it is challenging to simultaneously address time-varying dynamics and 
external disturbances without prior knowledge.
The predictive feature of Model Predictive Control (MPC) is a promis-
ing control approach for engineering applications, therefore, MPC has 
been applied extensively in UAV [14–27]. In [17], MPC for multi-input 
and multi-output systems were investigated for various scenarios, to 
achieve the complex trajectories tracking. To achieve smooth trajectory 
tracking, a MPC algorithm was proposed in [18] based on constrained 
linear errors and an enhanced model with an integrator to eliminate 
tracking errors caused by the external disturbance. In order to suppress 
the external disturbance, a robust time-varying constrained MPC was 
presented in [19] for controlling both the position and attitude of the 
QUAV. These approaches not only reduce the complexity of the con-
troller by simplifying the optimization problem in the rolling optimiza-
tion process, but also make the stability analysis be easy. However, the 
truncation of high-order terms in model linearization causes the degra-
dation in trajectory tracking accuracy. In addition, the methods based on 
the linearized model might cause instability when the system operates 
at the point far from the pre-designed working point. To solve the model 
inaccuracy, the neural network model was proposed in MPC [20–26].

Network (FNN) model to address model inaccuracy, which achieved 
better tracking performance as compared to that of Conventional 
MPC(CMPC) based on the linearized model. In [27], a method was 
proposed for time-varying nonlinear systems, utilizing dual function-
ally independent neural networks: a predictive network that replaces 
traditional prediction models and a control network that replaces the 
rolling optimization process to directly determine the optimal system 
input. To address the problem of the system with time-varying and dis-
turbance, the disturbance observer (DO) method was proposed in [28], 
but it was diﬃcult to tune the parameters. The interval observer (IO) 
demonstrated the robustness in [29], but it required prior knowledge 
of the disturbance. If the range of the disturbance cannot be known in 
advance, it is diﬃcult to eﬀectively observe the upper and lower bounds 
of the disturbance. Reference [30] established an adaptive nonsingular 
terminal sliding mode observer to estimate the external disturbances 
within the appointed settling time, however, the control performance is 
highly dependent on the considerable number of parameters selection. 
In [31], a parameter estimation-based observers (PEBO) was proposed 
to reformulating the state observation as a problem of parameter esti-
mation using regressor equation. Based on generalized PEBO, a dynamic 
regressor extension and mixing (DREM) and least squares (LS), an adap-
tive observer is provided [32], but the convergence speed of the method 
is relatively slower than the Extended State Observer (ESO) method. The 
ESO has demonstrated nice performance to estimate the disturbance and 
provided the eﬀective way to treat inaccuracy and time-varying model 
when combined with diﬀerent types of controllers [33,34]. In [35], a 
Fast Finite Time Stable-Extended State Observer (FFTS-ESO) method 
was proposed to achieve high precision under the slow-varying or con-
stant disturbances. In [36], an ESO was designed to estimate the inaccu-
racy and external disturbances of the system, and the estimated values 
were introduced into the cost function of Adaptive MPC (AMPC) in or-
der to achieve improved performance. An CMPC with ESO was proposed 
in [37] to enhance the robustness to the external disturbance and sys-
tem inaccuracy. Distinct from [37], the dual neural networks with pre-
trained weight matrix and on-line adaption are proposed in this paper as 
the prediction model in MPC in order to obtain the nonlinear dynamic 
of the QUAV and suppress the mathematical model error caused by the 
time-varying factors, by this way, the performance is improved. To deal 
with the external disturbances like wind and internal uncertainty caused 
by the residual identiﬁcation error of dual neural networks, the ESO is 
designed, and the outputs of ESO are fed into the dual neural networks 
to compensate the eﬀect. The comparison results show the eﬀectiveness 
and the superiority of the proposed method.
The contributions of this paper are as follows:
(1) The dual neural networks predictive model conﬁgure is proposed in 
this paper for the ﬁrst time to identify the diﬀerent time scale dynam-
ics of velocity and attitude loops with only one hidden layer neural 
networks, and to achieve fast real time implementation on the com-
monly used control chip to avoid the complication using traditional 
single deep hidden layers neural network;
(2) ESO is integrated into the dual neural network predictive model in 
AMPCESO to compensate the external disturbance as well as the pos-
sible remaining model error, by this way, the problems caused by 
both inaccurate predictive model and the external disturbance are 
solved simultaneously;
(3) A new additive composite Lyapunov function is proposed for the ﬁrst 
time to prove the stability of both the ESO and the AMPC in the 
proposed scheme.
The rest of this paper is organized as follows: The control problem 
and the mathematical model of QUAV considering wind disturbance are 
given in Section 2. the AMPCESO is given in detail in Section 3. The 
simulation and comparison results are given in Section 4 to show the 
eﬀectiveness and the robustness of the proposed method. Conclusions 
are given in Section 5.

3.  AMPCESO algorithm based on neural network
In this paper, a robust controller based on the framework of MPC 
is designed for the tracking problem. To deal with the complication of 
using traditional single deep hidden layers neural networks, a dual neu-
ral networks predictive model with only one hidden layer to identify the 
diﬀerent time-scale dynamics of the velocity and attitude loops are used 
as the predictive model, which are online updated in order to achieve ac-
curate tracking. To deal with the inaccuracy caused by predictive model 
and external disturbance, the ESO is designed to estimate and the esti-
mated values are fed into the dual neural network predictive model and 
treated in the MPC framework.

5.  Conclusion
The methodology of this paper addresses the trajectory tracking 
problem for the QUAV, which particularly treats both the time-varying 
model and external disturbance. In the proposed method, the neural 
network predictive model is updated in an on-line manner to accurately 
predict the QUAV output. The internal inaccuracy and the external dis-
turbance are estimated by the ESO. Eventually, the AMPCESO is pro-
posed to guarantee the tracking performance of the QUAV with dis-
turbance. The additive composite Lyapunov stability theory is proved 
for the convergence of the tracking error. The results, including HIL 
Experiment, demonstrate the eﬀectiveness and the superiority of the 
proposed method. Future research will focus on exploring multi-agent 
cooperative control for UAV swarm under this framework, enhancing 
the performance of autonomous trajectory planning and tracking in the 
unfamiliar environment.
