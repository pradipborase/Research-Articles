Quadrotor slung-load (QSL) systems are commonly employed in aerial transportation tasks, where precise 
trajectory tracking and eпђАective load swing suppression are critical challenges. These objectives are challenged 
by model uncertainties and unknown environmental disturbances, such as wind, which can compromise system 
stability and control accuracy. This paper addresses these challenges by developing a novel control strategy 
for QSL systems, where the system dynamics are divided into the attitude, position, and swing angle of the 
slung-load. Wind disturbances, including turbulence and wind shear, are modeled to simulate realistic outdoor 
scenarios. The integration backstepping control method forms the basis of the controller design, the sliding mode 
control is added to enhance the control accuracy and robustness while reducing steady-state error. An improved 
extended disturbance observer (IEDO) is incorporated to estimate and compensate for unknown disturbances and 
model uncertainties. The stability of the proposed controller and the convergence of the disturbance observer 
are rigorously analyzed using Lyapunov theory. Simulation results demonstrate the eпђАectiveness of the proposed 
approach, achieving signiпђБcant reductions in trajectory tracking errors and slung-load swing, even under complex 
and varying disturbance conditions.
1. Introduction
1.1. Background and motivation
Currently, quadrotors have gained widespread usage in various 
пђБelds, owing to their low cost, flexible movement, and convenient take¬≠
off and landing capabilities [1]. They are employed for tasks such as 
target search [2], agricultural plant protection [3], film and television 
entertainment [4], and more [5,6]. The mobility oпђАered by quadrotors 
for hanging and transporting goods is unparalleled, enabling the de¬≠

other means of transportation. Moreover, the quadrotorвАЩs ability to per¬≠
form slung-load transportation [7] eliminates the need to consider the 
shape of transported materials [8], while also being unrestricted by 
traditional transportation modes [9] and ground conditions [10]. As a 
result, quadrotors have become an integral part of the logistics [11] and 
transportation industry [12,13].
The quadrotor slung-load (QSL) system is a highly complex [14], 
strongly coupled [15], and underactuated nonlinear system [16] with 
four inputs and eight outputs [17]. Due to these characteristics, the QSL

Although current research on the QSL system has achieved notable 
progress, several critical challenges remain. Most studies are co¬≠fined 
to indoor environments, where the impact of external disturbances and 
model uncertainties, common in outdoor operations [34], is often over¬≠

systems in outdoor scenarios, such disturbances and uncertainties can 
substantially degrade control performance. Additionally, existing stud¬≠

to mitigate swing angle oscillations, rather than directly addressing the 
swing angle control itself. The inherent two degrees of freedom in the 
swing angle signiпђБcantly increase system coupling, posing further chal¬≠
lenges to controller design.
1.3. Main contribution
Based on the above discussion, we develop a new nonlinear control 
method for the QSL system. The main contributions of this paper are as 
follows:
(1) A comprehensive wind field disturbance model, incorporating tur¬≠
bulence and wind shear eпђАects, is established to simulate the com¬≠
posite disturbances encountered during actual outdoor operations. 
The impact of system model uncertainties also accounts, for provid¬≠

(2) A dedicated swing angle controller is designed to suppress large am¬≠
plitude oscillations and high-frequency motions of the slung load. 
By actively damping the swing, the controller maintains the load 
within a stable range, minimizing the risk of destabilization. This 
design signiпђБcantly enhances the robustness and reliability of the 
QSL system, especially under dynamic maneuvers or external dis¬≠
turbances.
(3) An improved extended disturbance observer (IEDO) is developed 
based on sliding mode control principles. The IEDO extends tradi¬≠
tional low-frequency disturbances [33,35] to handle complex, time¬≠
varying disturbances and incorporates the hyperbolic tangent func¬≠
tion to reduce chattering eпђАects. The proposed observer demon¬≠
strates faster convergence and higher accuracy in disturbance esti¬≠
mation, as validated through Lyapunov stability analysis.
(4) A robust controller integrating an integral backstepping sliding 
mode structure is proposed. The design leverages a hyperbolic tan¬≠
gent sliding surface to mitigate chattering while preventing param¬≠
eter saturation. The integral term eпђАectively eliminates steady-state

In this paper, a nonlinear control strategy is proposed for mitigating 
slung-load swing and achieving quadrotor trajectory tracking based on 
an improved extended disturbance observer. The system is subjected to

turbulence model, and model uncertainty disturbance. An improved ex¬≠
tended disturbance observer is proposed to estimate and compensate 
for time-varying disturbances in real time, and the bounded stability 
of observation errors is demonstrated. Nonlinear decoupling controllers 
are devised for the three dynamic subsystems of quadrotor attitude, po¬≠
sition, and slung-load swing angle, while considering underactuation, 
employing the integral backstepping sliding mode method. A swing an¬≠

swing under composite disturbances. Simulation results indicate that the 
nonlinear control method for the QSL system based on the improved ex¬≠
tended disturbance observer is eпђАective and superior in the presence 
of compound disturbances. The estimated disturbance error is reduced, 
and the control accuracy of the system is improved compared to the

curacy and load swing suppression lead to more precise and stable flight 
operations, particularly in challenging environmental conditions, ulti¬≠

system. Furthermore, this method yields smoother control inputs, which 
not only reduces energy consumption but also minimizes actuator wear, 
ultimately contributing to increased system eпђГciency and extended op¬≠
