Predefined-time sliding mode 
control for position and attitude of 
quadrotor with variable exponent 
coefficients
Lifeng Hou1, Jianhua Zhang2, Zhanyang Yu2, Xinxin Wang2 & Cheng Siong Chin3
This paper presents a comprehensive study on predefined-time sliding mode control applied to 
quadrotor systems with variable exponent coefficients. The main goal is to achieve efficient control 
with predefined-time convergence to the desired target. Novel predefined-time sliding mode control 
and predefined-time neural networks sliding model control algorithms are developed to adapt to 
the dynamics of variable coefficients and ensure system stability. The stability analysis confirms 
their capability to achieve predefined-time convergence. Extensive numerical simulations on diverse 
nonlinear systems validate the superiority of the proposed approach over conventional sliding mode 
control and neural networks control.
Keywords  Predefined-time stability, Sliding mode control, Neural networks, Trajectory tracking
Quadrotor aerial vehicles (UAVs) have gained significant attention in recent years due to their versatility in 
applications such as surveillance, search and rescue, payload delivery, and environmental monitoring.  The 
effectiveness of UAV control strategies has a direct impact on overall system performance1. The control of 
nonlinear systems has garnered significant attention in various engineering and scientific fields due to their 
widespread applications in modern technology2–4. Nonlinear systems are known for their complex dynamic 
behavior and the presence of uncertainties, posing significant challenges in control design. To tackle these 
complexities and achieve robust and efficient control, researchers have explored various control methodologies5,6. 
Among these, sliding mode control has emerged as a powerful technique that provides excellent performance, 
especially for nonlinear systems7–9.
Sliding mode control involves the introduction of a sliding surface in the state space to guide the system 
states towards a desired target rapidly10,11. Its robustness against parameter variations and external disturbances 
makes it suitable for stabilizing complex systems12–14. However, conventional sliding mode control suffers from 
one limitation: the convergence time is typically unbounded, which may not be acceptable in time-critical 
applications such as autonomous vehicles or robotic systems15–17.
Predefined-time (PT) control18,19 is a novel control strategy that ensures convergence time in a strictly 
predefined settling time, independent of initial conditions. Unlike traditional control methods that linear 
feedback control only ensures asymptotic stability with infinite time, finite/fixed-time20 and predefined-time 
control guarantee that the system state will reach the target point exactly at or before a specified time. To address 
this limitation and enhance the applicability of sliding mode control, a relatively new control technique known as 
fixed-time sliding mode control (FT-SMC). There exist some predefined-time control method, such as arbitrary 
convergence21 and variable exponent coefficient22,23 have been developed. For arbitrary convergence control, 
there exist result21, comments24,25 and authors’ reply in26, proposed sufficient condition for Lyapunov stability 
given for arbitrary chosen time stable system. For variable exponent coefficient (VEC)22,23 and comment27, 
it is hard to get the bounded of the convergence time under different initial conditions. FT-SMC imposes a 
predefined convergence time constraint, ensuring that the system reaches the desired target within a specified 
finite time28. This unique feature makes FT-SMC an ideal choice for real-time control applications, where 
precise and timely control responses are essential. For instance, the work in29 proposes finite-time sliding mode 
control with mismatched disturbances, but the convergence time bound depends on system parameters and 
initial conditions. Predefined-time30, prescribed-time31 and our VEC method allows the user to predefine the

Nonetheless, real-world systems often exhibit variable exponent coefficients, introducing additional 
complexities in the control process. Examples of such systems include variable coefficient circuits, adaptive 
control systems, and time-varying systems. These variable exponent coefficients challenge the application 
of traditional FT-SMC methods, necessitating innovative solutions to tackle this class of control problems. 
Therefore, the primary focus of this research is to explore and apply FT-SMC to nonlinear systems with variable 
exponent coefficients. The objective is to develop an effective control strategy that can handle the challenges 
posed by such systems and achieve robust and predefined-time convergence to the desired set-point.
This paper presents a comprehensive investigation into the theoretical foundation of PT-SMC, analyzing 
its advantages and applicability in addressing nonlinear control problems. Subsequently, a novel PT-SMC 
algorithm will be derived, tailored specifically for nonlinear systems with variable exponent coefficients. The 
design principles and control strategies of the proposed algorithm will be thoroughly examined. Furthermore, 
the stability of the derived control algorithm will be rigorously analyzed using mathematical tools to prove its 
capability to achieve predefined-time convergence, independent of system uncertainties and initial conditions. 
Numerical simulations will be conducted on various nonlinear systems with variable exponent coefficients to 
validate the performance and effectiveness of the proposed PT-SMC approach. Comparisons with traditional 
sliding mode control methods will be presented to highlight the advantages of the proposed technique.
The outcome of this research is expected to provide an efficient control solution for nonlinear systems with 
variable exponent coefficients, showcasing the potential of PT-SMC in real-time applications. The developed 
methodology may find application in diverse fields, including power electronics, robotics, and automation, 
where fast and accurate control responses are crucial for achieving optimal system performance. Ultimately, this 
study aims to contribute to the advancement of control theory and promote the adoption of predefined-time 
sliding mode control in a wide range of engineering and scientific domains.
In comparison to existing fixed-time and predefined-time control schemes18–20, the main novelties of 
this work are threefold: Firstly, the proposed Variable Exponent Coefficient (VEC) function provides a more 
flexible framework for tuning the predefined convergence time, overcoming the conservatism of fixed-exponent 
methods. Secondly, the integration of VEC with both standard sliding mode control and neural network-based 
adaptive control offers a unified approach to handle system uncertainties and nonlinearities with a guaranteed 
settling time. Thirdly, the application to the full six-degree-of-freedom quadrotor model, including both position 
and attitude tracking, demonstrates the practical viability of the method in complex, underactuated systems, 
which is not fully explored in the aforementioned works.
The main contributions are summarized as following.
	(1)	 The predefined-time sliding mode control method is proposed by variable exponent coefficients. Neural 
networks sliding mode control is designed for quadrotor dynamic system, and the proposed control strate­
gy guarantees that the PT stable.
	(2)	 Based on neural networks sliding mode control and Lyapunov theory, the close-loop system can be proved 
PT stable. The variable exponent coefficients adaptive law is proposed for neural networks.
	(3)	 The sliding mode PT control scheme is proposed for quadrotor, which refers to a control strategy where the 
system reaches the desired state within a predefined and finite time, regardless of initial conditions. This 
approach ensures convergence in a fixed time frame, enhancing predictability and robustness in dynamic 
systems.
The rest of the paper consists of the following parts. In “System description and problem formulation” section, 
mathematical description of quadrotor dynamic system and some basic definitions are presented. In “Sliding 
mode control with VEC” section, the sliding mode PT control with VEC is presented. In “Neural Networks 
sliding mode control with VEC” section, the neural networks sliding mode control is proposed for quadrotor 
dynamic system is proposed. In “Simulation example” section, sliding mode control and quadrotor dynamic 
system neural networks control results are illustrated by simulation examples. In “Conclusion” section, a 
conclusion is summarized.

uration. This issue can be effectively addressed by employing saturation limits or low-pass filters within the 
control algorithm.

Conclusion
This paper has proposed a novel predefined-time sliding mode control strategy utilizing Variable Exponent 
Coefficients (VEC) for the trajectory tracking of quadrotor UAVs. The primary contributions of this work are: 
(1) The development of a VEC-based sliding manifold that ensures predefined-time convergence regardless of 
initial conditions; (2) The design of an adaptive neural network controller integrated with the VEC framework 
to compensate for complex uncertainties without compromising the predefined-time stability property; (3) The 
successful application and validation of the proposed control scheme on a high-fidelity, nonlinear quadrotor 
model, demonstrating superior performance in tracking complex 3D trajectories.
The application of FT-SMC to nonlinear systems with VEC proves to be an effective and robust control 
strategy. The developed algorithm with VEC demonstrates remarkable adaptability in handling predefined-
