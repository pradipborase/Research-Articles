Abstract
Quadcopters are widely used in many fields due to their unique characteristics and simple structure. However, controlling 
a quadcopter remains challenging because it is an underactuated system. The main difficulties lie in achieving precise 
trajectory tracking and maintaining stability under disturbances and parameter uncertainties. This work investigates the 
development of a Fractional Order Synergetic Control (FOSC) approach optimized by the Equilibrium Optimizer (EO) 
algorithm to enhance the control performance of quadcopters under uncertain and disturbed conditions. The proposed 
method combines the advantages of fractional-order calculus with synergetic control theory, offering greater tuning flex¬≠
ibility, faster convergence, and reduced chattering. The controller parameters are automatically adjusted using the EO to 
improve optimal dynamic behavior. Closed-loop stability is verified through the Lyapunov direct method. To evaluate the 
effectiveness of the proposed FOSC technique, several simulations are carried out under different situations, including 
external disturbances, parametric uncertainties, and mass variation. A comparative study is also conducted to show the 
improvements of the proposed controller in tracking accuracy, response speed, and robustness. The obtained results con¬≠
firm that the proposed FOSC provides superior trajectory tracking and disturbance rejection compared with conventional 
control approaches.

However, despite their numerous benefits, quadcopters face

The nonlinearity of the system and the coupling dynamics 
make controlling a quadcopter a complicated and tough task. 
These factors add to the complexity and make this study 
topic both fascinating and challenging. Many control tech¬≠

Sliding Mode Control is a nonlinear control approach 
based on adjusting the system dynamics to follow a pre¬≠
defined sliding surface. It ensures strong robustness regard¬≠
ing disturbances. However, its primary limitation is the 
chattering phenomenon caused by quick switching of the 
control signal within that surface. Reference [27] presents a 
dual-channel disturbance rejection control technique using 
SMC and an Extended State Observer (ESO) for quadro¬≠
tor attitude regulation. This structure enhances robustness 
and improves disturbance estimation accuracy. However, 
the technique indicates a lack of fractional-order flexibil¬≠
ity, potentially constraining tuning adaptability in complex 
situations. Researchers in [28] proposed an adaptive SMC 
strategy augmented with a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) 
neural network for quadcopters. However, their approach 
still relies on integer-order dynamics, which limits tun¬≠
ing flexibility and can cause chattering under large distur¬≠
bances. The application of fractional calculus in control 
engineering has lately surged, due to its capacity to pro¬≠
vide more freedom and robustness relative to traditional 
integer-order methods. In [29], the authors investigated the 
implementation of two advanced controllers, namely the 
SMC and the FOSMC, applied to both integer- and frac¬≠
tional-order quadrotor systems. The study aimed to improve 
the control performance, robustness, and accuracy of the 
quadrotor by exploiting the benefits of fractional calculus in 
control design. The work presented in [30] integrates Frac¬≠
tional-Order Linear Active Disturbance Rejection Control 
(FOLADRC) with an enhanced Sparrow Search Algorithm 
(SSA) to form a robust attitude stabilization strategy for 
quadcopter UAV. This hybrid design leverages the distur¬≠
bance rejection capabilities of LADRC and the tuning flex¬≠
ibility of fractional-order PID control. In reference [31], a 
novel Fractional-Order Global SMC (FOGSMC) method 
was introduced for improving trajectory tracking of quad¬≠
copter UAVs operating in uncertain and disturbed environ¬≠
ments. By constructing a fractional-order sliding manifold, 
the approach ensures enhanced robustness and tracking 
precision. The authors of the work presented in [32] intro¬≠

improve disturbance rejection and system responsiveness. 
In the work presented in [33], a Fixed-Time Fractional-
Order SMC (FTFOSMC) strategy was developed to provide 
accurate trajectory tracking under external disturbances and 
system uncertainties.
Research on Synergetic Control (SC) for quadcopters is 
not widespread, making it a lot of potential development. 
SC avoids chattering, adapts to disturbances, and main¬≠

The quadrotor features four propellers arranged in a cross 
configuration, with two pairs rotating in opposing directions. 
Altering rotor speeds enables the modification of lift forces 
and the generation of motion. Consequently, uniformly aug¬≠
menting or diminishing the velocities of the four propellers 
produces vertical movement. Adjusting the speeds of the 
2 and 4 propellers inversely results in roll rotation accom¬≠
panied by lateral movement. Pitch rotation and the associ¬≠
ated lateral movement arise from the inverse modification 
of the speeds of propellers 1 and 3. Yaw rotation is more 
nuanced, as it arises from the disparity in counter-torque 
between each set of propellers. The quadcopter configura¬≠
tion is shown in Fig.¬†1.

3.1‚Äā Control architecture
The quadcopter system includes 4 direct control inputs, 
U1, U2, U3 and U4, in addition to 2 virtual controllers, Ux 
and Uy, with 6 degrees of freedom. The system is divided 
into 2 cascade subsystems: one for the position control, 
which corresponds to the outer loop, and the second for 
the attitude control, which corresponds to the inner loop. 
By separating the position and attitude dynamics, the quad¬≠
copter is split into two coupled subsystems. The rotation 
dynamics are not impacted by the translation components; 
however, the translational dynamics are impacted by angles. 
The signal U1 controls the altitude, while the two additional 
translation variables, x and y, are controlled indirectly by 
controlling the 2 rotation angles (ŌÜ and őł) using the virtual 
controllers Ux and Uy. The control structure is shown in
