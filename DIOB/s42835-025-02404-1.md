Abstract
This work is an investment in addressing the optimal trajectory tracking control problem for a quadrotor unmanned aerial 
vehicle (UAV) subjected to external disturbance. The proposed method is a combination of an Adaptive Dynamic Pro¬≠
gramming (ADP)-based controller and a nonlinear disturbance observer (NDO), ensuring the stability of the closed-loop 
quadrotor under the influence of unknown external disturbance and achieving near-optimal trajectory tracking control. 
Firstly, the tracking error dynamics are established by utilizing the feedforward terms to compensate for the known 
time-varying error information in the quadrotor dynamics. Then, a nonlinear disturbance observer (NDO) is designed to 
compensate for unknown external disturbances. By eliminating the influence of external disturbances and time-varying 
dynamics in the quadrotor system, the error model of the quadrotor is transferred to the nominal model. A critic-only 
learning structure is applied for both position and attitude controllers to learn the optimal feedback control policy. Finally, 
a flight simulation is given to illustrate the superior efficiency of the proposed control scheme. The simulation result 
shows that the proposed method lies in its ability to compute a smooth optimal control policy even under the influence 
of disturbances, while effectively mitigating the chattering effect.

In the control systems engineering community, opti¬≠
mal control has always attracted significant attention and 
remains a highly influential research topic [11вАУ13]. Theo¬≠
retically, the optimal control problem typically involves 
solving the Algebraic Riccati Equation (ARE) for linear 
systems [14] and the Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman (HJB) equa¬≠
tion for nonlinear systems [15]. However, it is more diffi¬≠
cult or impossible to find the optimal solution of the HJB 
equation for highly nonlinear systems such as quadrotor 
UAVs using only mathematical analysis [16]. To overcome 
this challenge, in recent years, reinforcement learning (RL)-
based control approaches have provided more considerable 
advantages to solve optimal control problems, which find 
the optimal solution without directly solving the HJB equa¬≠
tion. The adaptive dynamic programming (ADP) technique 
is a class of RL that serves as a bridge between traditional 
optimal control methods and adaptive control algorithms. 
To the best of our knowledge, ADP techniques utilize neu¬≠
ral networks (NNs) as a feasible approach. The traditional 
ADP structure is the actor-critic (AC) structure, which was 
developed by Lewis et al. for nonlinear systems to solve 
optimal regulation control [17] and optimal tracking con¬≠
trol [18]. According to a predefined cost function, the critic 
NN is employed to approximate the cost function, while the 
actor NN is employed to approximate an improving con¬≠
trol policy. However, training two NNs typically requires 
significant computational resources, making it challeng¬≠
ing to implement the algorithm in practical applications. 
To overcome this limitation, Ouyang et al. [19] developed 
a critic-only learning structure that was superior to the AC 
structure in computation reduction when using only a NN. 
For both learning structures, a necessary condition called 
persistent excitation (PE) condition must be satisfied to 
guarantee the existence of weight update laws for NNs. 
However, the ADP technique performs poorly in handling 
external time-varying disturbances, thus failing to meet the 
performance requirements of disturbance attenuation when 
quadrotor UAVs fly in complex environments. As a result, 
this indicates that the high-quality control performance of 
practical quadrotor systems cannot be achieved only by the 
ADP technique.
The quadrotor UAV usually suffers the influence of 
unknown external disturbances from aerospace effects, 
including aerodynamic drag and lift forces, as well as 
friction forces [20]. As mentioned earlier, the ADP-based 
control is not able to deal with time-varying external distur¬≠
bances. Therefore, it usually requires rejecting the influence 
of external disturbance before implementing the ADP algo¬≠
rithm. The SMC can effectively robustly respond to external 
disturbances that satisfy bounded conditions. Zhao et al. 
[21] developed an SMC-based ADP controller to obtain 
approximate optimal control for a class of uncertain non¬≠
linear systems. However, as a common problem with SMC 
methods, chattering reduction must be considered. In con¬≠
trast, the disturbance observer (DO) method is considered to 
estimate and compensate for unknown external disturbances 
[22]. Under the assumption of a small-bounded disturbance 
change rate, the estimated disturbance error converges to 
a small-bounded neighborhood of zero and is set to zero. 
Duo et al. [23] introduced a robust controller constructed 
by an ADP controller and an adaptive disturbance observer 
(ADO). There, an ADO is employed to estimate the external 
disturbance with an unknown upper bound, and the ADP-
based controller is utilized to drive the tracking errors of 
the quadrotor UAV to zero. However, the problem of con¬≠
sidering the position and attitude dynamics as a nominal 
linear model subject to disturbance may result in a large 
upper-bounded disturbance, leading to significant estima¬≠
tion errors.
Inspired by previous works [23] and these previous dis¬≠
cussions, this paper proposes an ADP-based DO control¬≠
ler for the nonlinear quadrotor UAV subjected to unknown 
external time-varying disturbances. The main contribution 
is listed as follows:
1.	 Compared to [23], the approach implements the ADP-
based control for the quadrotor system to simplify the 
quadrotor dynamics as a nominal linear system sub¬≠
jected to disturbance. The proposed ADP-based control 
adopts a non-linear quadrotor dynamic model to learn 
the near-optimal solution.
2.	 The NDO is designed to eliminate the influence of 
unknown external time-varying disturbances in the 
quadrotor dynamics. Under the assumption of a small 
disturbance change rate, the external disturbance can 
be estimated and compensated accurately, and the esti¬≠
mated disturbance error converges to a small bounded 
neighborhood of zero and is set to zero.
3.	 The updated NN weight for critic-only learning is 
designed to learn the near-optimal solution online. The 
proposed ADP-based NDO controller achieves approx¬≠
imate optimal trajectory tracking and guarantees the 
stability of the quadrotor system under the influence of 
external disturbances.

based NDO algorithm. Section 4, the simulation results and 
analysis are provided. Finally, Sect. 5 concludes this paper
