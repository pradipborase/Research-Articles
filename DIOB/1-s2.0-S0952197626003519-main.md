A B S T R A C T
Autonomous aerial docking of Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) is essential for aerial refueling, payload 
replacement, and cooperative operations, yet existing methods often exhibit low docking accuracy, weak 
disturbance rejection, and empirical parameter tuning. To overcome these limitations, this study proposes an 
intelligent control framework that integrates Linear Active Disturbance Rejection Control (LADRC) with an 
Improved Particle Swarm Optimization (IPSO) algorithm. First, a six degree of freedom dynamic model of the 
UAV and cone sleeve system is developed, incorporating wind disturbance, turbulence, and parameter pertur¬≠
bations. Second, the LADRC method realizes decoupled control of altitude, lateral, and velocity channels, 
ensuring robust dynamic compensation. Third, the IPSO algorithm, an Artificial Intelligence (AI) based opti¬≠
mization approach, is employed to adaptively tune the controller bandwidth and observer gains. This AI 
enhanced parameter learning process improves the generalization capability of LADRC under varying flight 
conditions. Simulation and scaled flight experiments demonstrate that the proposed AI driven LADRC achieves 
stable docking under fifty percent perturbations, with a trajectory root mean square error of 0.04 m and a relative 
velocity error of 0.03 m per second. Compared with conventional controllers, the tracking error is reduced by up 
to 38 percent. These results confirm that combining LADRC with AI based optimization offers a robust and 
precise solution for UAV autonomous aerial docking in complex and uncertain environments.
1. Introduction
In recent years, with the rapid development of microelectronic sen¬≠
sors (Covaciu and Iordan, 2022), computer vision (Akbari et al., 2021), 
and artificial intelligence technologies (Bakirci, 2025), the application 
scope of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) has been continuously 
expanding in both military and civilian domains. Owing to their ad¬≠
vantages of simple structure, high maneuverability, and low operational 
cost, UAVs have been widely employed in missions such as search and 
rescue (Pereira, 2021), cargo transportation (Meincke, 2021), environ¬≠
mental monitoring (Liao et al., 2021), communication relay (Yin et al., 
2023), agricultural operations (Subeesh and Mehta, 2021), and infra¬≠
structure inspection (Raja et al., 2022). These applications have greatly 
improved operational efficiency and mission flexibility. Particularly in 
the fields of public safety and emergency response, UAVs can be rapidly 
deployed to perform various tasks, including providing aerial commu¬≠
nication coverage, delivering emergency supplies, and conducting 
disaster monitoring, demonstrating their irreplaceable role in disaster 
management and emergency command.
However, the limited endurance of UAVs remains a critical bottle¬≠
neck restricting their ability to perform long-duration and large-scale 
operations. At present, most UAVs still rely on lithium-ion or lithium- 
polymer batteries as their main power source. Due to the constraints 
of energy density, simply increasing battery capacity leads to higher 
structural weight, reduced payload capability, and potential risks such 
as decreased efficiency and safety concerns (Caballero-Martin et al., 
2024). Studies have shown that when the battery weight accounts for 
approximately two-thirds of the UAV's total mass, the endurance reaches 
its theoretical peak. Therefore, merely enlarging the battery capacity 
cannot fundamentally enhance endurance performance. To address this

adjust their motion according to real-time changes in relative distance 
and speed to accomplish a successful docking. This process imposes 
extremely high demands on the UAV's control system in terms of dy¬≠

Currently, extensive research has been conducted both domestically 
and internationally in the field of autonomous aerial docking for un¬≠
manned aerial vehicles. In 2021, the research team led by Basilio Caruso 
(Caruso et al., 2021) at the Florida Institute of Technology, Brazil (P2‚ó¶) 
proposed an innovative unmanned aerial collaboration technology. The 
system integrates a fixed-wing mothership with multi-rotor drones, 
equipped with a Rescue‚ÄďDelivery (RED) system, enabling rapid, precise, 
and fully autonomous transportation of payloads from a base to a rescue 
site. During the simulation phase, both manual static docking and dy¬≠
namic docking tests were successfully conducted, verifying the stability 
and overall reliability of the proposed docking mechanism. In 2022, 
Liang Wang and colleagues from Fudan University (Wang et al., 2022) 
developed an autonomous docking and landing trajectory tracking 
controller based on Radial Basis Function (RBF) Proportio¬≠
nal‚ÄďIntegral‚ÄďDerivative (PID) control. The system incorporates a 
vision-guided module employing YOLOv3 for real-time target detection, 
enabling a position update rate of eight frames per second during aerial 
docking and landing. Experimental results demonstrated that the pro¬≠
posed approach enables autonomous aerial docking and precise landing 
of multirotor UAVs, confirming its feasibility and stability in complex 
operational environments. Liu Y (Liu et al., 2020) proposed a 
data-driven docking safety assessment and optimization method to 
improve AAR docking safety and success rate by extracting information 
from docking data using deep learning methods. Simulation results show 
that these methods significantly improve the autonomous aerial refu¬≠
eling docking accuracy and optimize the anti-jamming capability. 
However, these methods share common drawbacks. Most rely on 
idealized simulations and lack real-world validation under complex 
environmental conditions. They also depend heavily on accurate 
modeling and parameter tuning, resulting in limited disturbance rejec¬≠
tion. Moreover, coordination of relative velocity and position is insuf¬≠
ficiently addressed, making stable docking difficult under strong 
nonlinear disturbances.
In recent years, the ADRC control method (Madonski et al., 2023) has 
been widely used in UAV flight control. Its advantages include real-time 
estimation and compensation of load effects, adaptive control without 
the need of an accurate model, good robustness against uncertainties 
and perturbations, and enhancement of flight stability and adaptability 
by cross-scale control to improve system adaptability and dynamic 
response. In 2019, Chaohai Kang and colleagues from Northeast Petro¬≠
leum University (Kang et al., 2019)developed an ADRC module library 
based on the concept of modular design. They proposed a hybrid opti¬≠
mization algorithm that combines the Artificial Fish Swarm Algorithm 
(AFSA) and the Standard Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) to enhance 
parameter tuning. MATLAB simulation results verified the effectiveness 
and superiority of the ADRC controller optimized by this hybrid algo¬≠
rithm in terms of control performance. In 2022, Bohai Deng and col¬≠
leagues from Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics (Deng 
and Xu, 2022) proposed a control approach based on an Extended State 
Observer (ESO) to estimate and compensate for the internal un¬≠
certainties and external disturbances of unmanned aerial vehicles, 
thereby improving control accuracy. Meanwhile, a Genetic Algo¬≠
rithm‚ÄďParticle Swarm Optimization (GA‚ÄďPSO) technique was employed 
to jointly optimize the controller parameters, further enhancing the 
system's dynamic performance and robustness. In 2023, Ruiyang Zhou 
and Konstantin A. Neusypin (Zhou and Neusypin, 2023) from Bauman 
Moscow State Technical University developed a cascade control scheme 
based on Linear Active Disturbance Rejection Control (LADRC) for 
fixed-wing unmanned aerial vehicles. In this scheme, three ADRC con¬≠
trollers are employed for attitude control, while two additional ADRC 
controllers are used for heading and altitude tracking. The results 
demonstrated that the proposed control approach exhibits excellent 
robustness and high accuracy, even under conditions of strong external 
disturbances. Although the above methods have achieved satisfactory 
control performance, most of them rely on accurate mathematical 
modeling of the UAV (Saranya et al., 2023), which may lead to reduced 
disturbance rejection capability in complex and uncertain environ¬≠
ments. In addition, these methods feature relatively complex control 
structures and fail to fully address the coordinated control requirements 
of relative velocity and relative position during the UAV aerial docking 
process.
Based on the above analysis, it can be concluded that in the process 
of autonomous aerial docking for unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), 
achieving a gradual reduction in relative distance through accel¬≠
eration‚ÄĒso that both the relative distance and relative velocity between 
the UAV and the target approach zero‚ÄĒis a key challenge that must be 
addressed to ensure smooth and reliable docking. Existing research still 
faces limitations in docking accuracy, system stability, and disturbance 
rejection capability, particularly under complex aerodynamic distur¬≠
bances and parameter uncertainties, where the robustness and adapt¬≠
ability of UAV control systems remain insufficient.
To address these issues, this paper proposes an intelligent control 
framework integrating Linear Active Disturbance Rejection Control 
(LADRC) with an Improved Particle Swarm Optimization (IPSO) algo¬≠
rithm, aiming to achieve high-precision control and robust stability 
during UAV autonomous aerial docking. A six-degree-of-freedom 
nonlinear dynamic model of the UAV‚Äďdrogue system is first estab¬≠
lished, incorporating wind disturbances, atmospheric turbulence, and 
parameter perturbations to provide a realistic foundation for controller 
design. Then, a hierarchical decoupled control strategy based on LADRC 
is developed to coordinate the control of altitude, lateral, and velocity 
channels while performing dynamic disturbance compensation. On this 
basis, an artificial intelligence‚Äďbased IPSO algorithm is introduced to 
adaptively optimize the control parameters, enabling the system to 
perceive environmental variations and perform self-learning, thereby 
improving control accuracy and stability during docking. Finally, both 
simulation and real flight validation confirm the feasibility and effec¬≠
tiveness of the proposed control method, providing new theoretical and 
technical support for the intelligent and practical development of UAV

2.5. Analysis of UAV airborne autonomous docking flight process
Using the UAV body coordinate system (Jurkus et al., 2023) as a 
reference, the taper sleeve was set to be 30 m above and 100 m to the left 
of the UAV. The UAV needs to complete docking with the cone sleeve 
through two control stages. This paper analyzes the two phases of po¬≠
sition control and accelerated docking control during the UAV flight, as 
shown in Figs. 6 and 7.
The first stage is UAV position control. The constant speed level flight 
mode is selected to keep the UAV speed constant, complete the longi¬≠
tudinal and lateral position adjustment, and realize the coupled altitude 
and lateral deviation control. The UAV needs to maintain the same di¬≠
rection, the same height and no lateral deviation with the taper sleeve, 
while the taper sleeve continues to fly in a straight line at a constant 
speed. The second stage is accelerated docking control. The UAV flies in 
an accelerated state and starts to decelerate after a certain distance until 
the speed is zero. During this process, it is necessary to ensure that both 
the relative distance and the relative velocity difference are zero. When 
these two differences are zero, the UAV successfully docks with the cone 
sleeve and then continues to fly at the same speed. Based on the above 
analysis, the UAV aerial docking flowchart is established, as shown in

3. LADRC-based UAV air-docking control design
3.1. UAV air docking flight control law design
Since PID and LQR controllers perform well in linear systems but 
have limitations in nonlinear systems and are susceptible to external 
disturbances, difficult parameter tuning, and dependent on accurate 
modeling, this paper adopts the LADRC control method to design the 
flight control law. Considering the coupling and nonlinear characteris¬≠
tics of the UAV in longitudinal and transverse directions, the control 
design is divided into inner and outer loops, in which the inner loop uses 
the eigenstructure configuration method to increase the stability, and 
the outer loop adopts the LADRC control method to control the position, 
relative speed and relative distance. The control structure is shown in 
Fig. 9. The altitude and side deviation control are used as inputs to the 
outer loop, and are fed into the LADRC inner loop controller through the 
UAV's altitude/side deviation commands and feedback, and the inner 
loop outputs rudder commands to realize attitude control. Velocity 
control, on the other hand, inputs the velocity difference between the 
UAV and the taper sleeve through the LADRC controller, and adjusts the 
UAV to accelerate and then decelerate until the relative distance and 
relative velocity are both zero, thus completing the velocity docking 
control.

6. Conclusion
This study proposed an intelligent control framework for Unmanned 
Aerial Vehicle (UAV) autonomous aerial docking to overcome the lim¬≠
itations of low docking accuracy, weak disturbance rejection, and 
empirical parameter tuning observed in conventional approaches. First, 
a six-degree-of-freedom nonlinear dynamic model of the UAV and the 
towed cone sleeve was developed, comprehensively incorporating wind 
disturbance, atmospheric turbulence, and parameter uncertainties, 
which provides a rigorous foundation for controller design. Second, a 
hierarchical decoupled control strategy was established, where a first- 
order Linear Active Disturbance Rejection Control (LADRC) regulates 
altitude and lateral motion, and a second-order LADRC governs velocity 
dynamics, achieving dynamic channel decoupling and real-time distur¬≠
bance compensation. Finally, an artificial intelligence optimization 
method, namely the Improved Particle Swarm Optimization (IPSO) al¬≠
gorithm, was introduced to adaptively tune the controller bandwidths 
and observer gains. This intelligent optimization mechanism enables the 
control system to perceive environmental variations, perform self- 
learning, and maintain robustness and stability under dynamic 
conditions.
Both simulation and real flight experiments were conducted to verify 
the effectiveness of the proposed framework. The results confirm that 
the AI-optimized LADRC controller achieves high-precision trajectory 
tracking and stable docking performance under atmospheric distur¬≠
bances and ¬Ī50% parameter perturbations. The system demonstrates 
excellent stability, smooth transient response, and strong disturbance 
rejection, validating the engineering feasibility and practical applica¬≠
bility of the proposed control approach.
Although the proposed framework has achieved promising results, 
several limitations of the LADRC model remain. Specifically, the LADRC 
assumes that external disturbances can be fully estimated and 
compensated within the observer bandwidth, which may not hold under 
high-frequency turbulence, actuator delays, or strong subsystem 
coupling. In addition, the current formulation does not explicitly 
consider actuator saturation and communication latency, which may 
influence real-time response in more complex outdoor scenarios.
Future work will focus on extending the LADRC framework toward 
enhanced autonomy and adaptability. Specifically, we will develop 
high-fidelity aerodynamic coupling models to capture the unsteady ef¬≠
fects of wake vortices, perform large-scale outdoor flight experiments to 
validate the controller under variable wind fields, and integrate deep 
reinforcement learning‚Äďbased adaptive control strategies for multi-UAV 
cooperative docking and mission reconfiguration. These advancements 
aim to further enhance the intelligence, robustness, and scalability of 
UAV autonomous docking systems.
